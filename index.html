Representing Native Americans

The Georgia Historical Quarterly

For the past one hundred years, the Georgia Historical Society (GHS) has published a collection of scholarly articles, book reviews, essays, primary sources, and oral histories that they consider imperative to Georgia's history called The Georgia Historical Quarterly (GHQ). Through the use of textual analysis, this project explores how imperative the GHS believes Native Americans are to Georgia's history by examining how Native Americans are portrayed in the GHQ. The use of textual analysis on every five years of publications of the GHQ reveals not only how often the GHS has deemed Native Americans important enough to Georgia's history to include them in the GHQ but also if the GHS only deems Native Americans important when they advance the story of Europeans and Anglo-Americans. By identifying how the GHS represents Native Americans in the GHQ, one can also gain a better understanding of Native Americans' place in Georgians' historical memories.
