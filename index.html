Representing Native Americans

The Georgia Historical Quarterly

For the past one hundred years, the Georgia Historical Society (GHS) has published a collection of scholarly articles, book reviews, essays, primary sources, and oral histories that they consider imperative to Georgia's history called The Georgia Historical Quarterly (GHQ). This project attempts to analyze how imperative the GHS believes Native Americans are to Georgia's history by using various forms of textual analysis on every five years of publications of the GHQ to examine how often it mentions Native Americans, which tribes it mentions most, and what context it mentions Native Americans in. The textual analysis performed in this project reveals how often the GHS has deemed Native Americans important enough to Georgia's history to include in the GHQ, as well as if the historical society only deems Native Americans important when they advance the story of Europeans and Anglo-Americans. By exploring how the GHS portrays Native Americans in the GHQ, one can also gain a better understanding of Native Americans' places in Georgians' historical memories.
